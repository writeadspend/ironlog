When and how much to add

▾
The rule: hit every rep of every set with good form → add weight next session. Miss any reps → stay at the same weight. The tracker tells you which applies.

What to do when you stall

▾
Stall = missing reps two sessions in a row on the same lift. This is normal and inevitable. The tracker detects it automatically and tells you what to do.
  • 1Drop that lift back 10% (round to nearest 2.5 lbs)
  • 2Rebuild, adding weight each session as normal
  • 3You'll often push past your old sticking point on the way back up
  • 4Stall again at the same weight? Drop 15% instead and rebuild
Stalling means you're pushing hard enough to actually adapt. It's a sign of progress, not failure.

Form beats weight, every time

▾
A rep that looked or felt wrong doesn't count. An injury sets you back weeks. Lifting 5 lbs lighter sets you back nothing.
  • →Record yourself from the side occasionally — you'll catch things you can't feel
  • →If a rep is a grind to finish, that's your last rep. Rack it.
  • →Rest 2–3 minutes between heavy sets. Impatience is how form breaks down.
  • →Soreness just means novelty — it fades after a few weeks. That's normal.
